# Thesis

I'm a builder. Not in the Silicon Valley sense of the word — in the older sense. I make things that weren't there before, and what makes them worth making is how they change the lives of the people who use them.

I lead by doing. Show people what good looks like, give them the vision and the room to figure out the rest. Trust them first — don't make them earn what should be freely given. When things go wrong between people, clear the air immediately. Festering is worse than friction.

I hold hard lines on ethics. Some things are simply wrong and context doesn't change that. But within those lines, I stay open. Open to people, to ideas, to being wrong. I learn from everything — doing, reading, watching others, seeing enough examples that the pattern emerges on its own.

Money is a tool for freedom and a responsibility to share. Competition is mostly with myself; I'd rather grow the pie than fight over slices. The world has deep problems — broken incentives, fractured truth, short-term thinking, learned helplessness — and they're all connected. I see them clearly and choose to build anyway. Progress is real, but it's fragile, and it requires active effort from people who refuse to be cynical.

I protect rest like I protect principles — fiercely, without apology. Not because I'm afraid of burning out, but because the infinite game demands showing up whole. I've been in the spotlight enough to know fame is noise. What matters is respect from people whose judgment I trust, and work that speaks for itself.

I raise my kids the way I lead my teams: give them roots and wings, model the behavior, prepare them for a hard world, and then get out of the way and let them become who they're going to become.
